Get 10% of car users onto the school bus

Transport is our biggest CO₂ source by far. If 38 students switched from private cars to the school bus, we'd save 210 kg of CO₂ every week. The eco-club can make a bus route guide and put it on the notice board.

Turn off AC 30 minutes after the last bell

Our energy data shows electricity keeps being used after school hours. A simple rule — or a student "energy monitor" role — to switch off AC when classrooms are empty could cut 128 kg of CO₂ per week.

Saves 128 kg CO₂/week Needs principal approval ₹2,000 one-time cost
3

Water the school garden before 7am only

Garden water use has grown 76% in 8 weeks. Watering early morning (5–7am) reduces evaporation and can cut water use by 40%. That's about 4,000 litres saved every week with just a schedule change.

Saves 4,000L water/week Easy to start Zero cost
4

Start a "Green Commuter" recognition board

15% of students already walk or cycle — above the national average. A simple notice board celebrating walkers and cyclists each month can inspire more students to switch. Target: grow from 15% to 20%.

Saves 70 kg CO₂/week Very easy ₹500/month
About this AI tool — what it can and can't do
⚠️

Known limitation

All school data is synthetic (AI-generated). It represents a typical Bengaluru school but may not match your school's exact situation. Real numbers could differ.

🔍

How we're honest

Every number shows where it came from. We use India's official electricity emission factor (0.82 kg CO₂/kWh from CEA 2023). All assumptions are visible.

👩‍🏫

Humans decide

The AI never takes action. It only suggests. Every recommendation must be reviewed and approved by a teacher or school operations staff before anything changes.
